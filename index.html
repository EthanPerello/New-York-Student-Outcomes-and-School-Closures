New York Student Outcomes and School Closures¶

Ethan Perello and Adam Goodman¶

Introduction¶

This collaborative project between Ethan Perello and Adam Goodman will be investigating data collected on high school student outcomes, and school closure statistics to assess the factors that cause schools to close and understand which schools are at risk of closure in New York.

The school closures dataset identifies which schools have closed in 2015 and 2016. The 2015 closures are from https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Education/2014-2015-School-Closure-Discharge-Reporting-GPA/qd93-w582 in a .csv format and the 2016 closures are copied from an online document at https://infohub.nyced.org/docs/default-source/default-document-library/201516schoolclosuredischargereportingnarrative.pdf. We wanted to explore more recent school closure data, but 2015 and 2016 was the most recent that we could find.

The student outcomes dataset contains graduation rates and outcomes for students in different subgroups of race, economic status, sex, disability, and english learning for each school in 2021. This dataset comes from https://data.nysed.gov/downloads.php in a .csv format. Each observation is a subgroup of a graduating class at one high school.

We chose to explore school closure data because many schools are closed in the U.S and school closure can have a negative effect on the students at closed schools. According to the Manhattan Institue for Policy Research: "In many cases, schools are closed in response to declining populations or other factors that led to a substantial decline in available resources. In other cases, school boards and elected officials struggle with the decision of what to do with persistently ineffective schools." 1 Being able to understand what factors cause closures could help prevent school closures. This would help many students and save resources.

We chose to explore student outcome data because student outcomes are an indicator of student success. We suspect that there may be a relationship between students outcomes and school closures and we wanted to see how schools that closed performed relative to other schools. If there actually is a relationship between student outcomes and school closures, we could use a supervised machine learning model to predict schools that are at risk of closing. It is important to acknowledge that many schools closed are charter schools that closed due to financial reasons, but we still believe a relationship may exist between student outcomes and closures.

1 https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED599013.pdf

Questions:

How do different subgroups of students perform relative to each other?

How related are student outcomes and whether or not a school is closed?

Can student outcome data for subgroups of students be used to identify which schools are at risk of closing?

We are trying to solve the problem of having missing school closure data in 2021 by using student outcome data from 2021 and 2015 of the same metrics.

Conclusions¶

How do different subgroups of students perform relative to each other?

Student outcomes in New York differ greatly between subgroups. Female students are significantly more likely to graduate than male students. White students are significantly more likely to graduate than black or hispanic students.

How related are student outcomes and school closure?

Some student outcome variables are somewhat correlated with school closure, but none have a very high correlation and some have a very low correlation. Correlations vary quite a bit between subgroups. Variables for the 'Black or African American' subgroup seem to have a higher correlation than variables for other racial subgroups.

Can student outcome data for subgroups of students be used to identify which schools are at risk of closing?

Our model using student outcome data for subgroups of students was very effective at predicting the 2015 schools that closed when performing cross-validation. Our model was somewhat confident when predicting which 2021 schools were at risk of closing, but not confident enough where the predictions should be trusted. While the predictions cannot fully be trusted, they do provide insight because the schools predicted to close were very similar to the schools closed in 2015.
