Identifying Schools at Risk of Closure In New York¶

Ethan Perello and Adam Goodman¶

This collaborative project between Ethan Perello and Adam Goodman will be investigating data collected on high school student outcomes, student behavior, and school funding to assess the factors that cause schools to close and understand which schools are at risk of closure.

Plan¶

1) Load in data for schools in 2021

2) Load in data for schools from 2015 and 2016 and identify schools that closed

3) Train classification model on schools from 2015 and 2016 with closed as the target variable

4) Check effectiveness of model on training data

5) Use the model to predict which schools from 2021 are at risk of closing

6) Analyze the schools that are at risk compared to the average school

Examining 2021 Student Outcomes and Behavior and School Funding Data¶

Graduation Rates Data¶

First, load the graduation rates data into a pandas dataframe. This dataset contains graduation rates and outcomes for students in different subgroups of race, economic status, sex, disability, and english learning for each school. This dataset comes from https://data.nysed.gov/downloads.php in a cvs format. Each observation is a the graduating class of a subgroup at one high school. The team chose this data set because failing to meet graduation outcomes are often cited for reasons a school has closed. We want to learn if there is a cutoff point for administration and the weight of student outcomes in closing schools.

def plot_subgroups(subgroups):
  df = grad_rate_df[["aggregation_name", 'subgroup_name', "grad_pct"]]
  for subgroup in subgroups:
    indices=[]
    for i in range(0,len(df)):
      if df.iloc[i].subgroup_name == subgroup:
        indices.append(i)
    grad_pcts = df.iloc[indices]['grad_pct']
    plt.hist(grad_pcts, bins=20, alpha=0.2, label=subgroup)
  plt.legend(loc='upper right')
  plt.show()
